Final System

We created an autonomous garden system that will water and deliver light to your plants using light and soil moisture sensors. Easily take care of your plants by just filling up a water container and then just letting the system do the work for you!

Videos of the System

These are videos of the different parts of the system working separately. We will be uploading a final video on Wednesday; stay tuned for an exciting update!

These videos show the lights responding to control, the pump responding to control, and an led showing the water level and when to refill the tank respectfully.

Overall System Diagram

How We Got Here

Sprint One

For the first sprint we focused on irrigation control, for just one plant controlled exclusively by gravity (without any pump). We wanted to start small and with the scariest part (the water), so we decided to keep it simple for this sprint.
Sprint Two

For the second sprint we tried to get the control code working with two sensors and two plants and the light control working for these two as well. We hoped that we could add another plant in easily (which we could) and wanted to get started working on the other major part of our system, the lights.
Sprint Three

For the third sprint we focused on getting the control working, which meant working on getting the Wire library working. From the previous two sprints we realized that we could not make the system as we wanted to without two arduinos, so we worked to get the dual arduino system working and learning all we could about the libraries necessary. We made a lot of design decisions based on this as well (where to house the electronics and how to power the different systems).
Final Week

During the last week, we focused on putting everything together and doing testing so that it would work for a long time and keep all your plants alive! We also worked on making the website that you're reading right now and getting all of our documentation together.
