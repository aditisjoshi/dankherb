Mechanical System

Mechanical

The mechanical system consists mainly of the box structure, the design of which includes waterproofing, aesthetics, function, and accessibility, and the irrigation system, which ensures that water reaches at the correct pressure and without interfering with other parts of the system.

We designed the project to be modular, easily configurable, and readily variable to allow for quick adaptation and universality of use. It is also intended to be long lasting (relative to most PoE projects), and we hope itâ€™s life span extends into the coming months or years.

Structure

The box structure consists of a non-structural but aesthetically pleasing wrap around exterior, an interior casing, top, bottom, and three laser cut and cemented acrylic boxes. The MDF interior casing serves to hold

  • all of our electronics high above the water in the first acrylic box,
  • fresh water ready to be pumped into the plants,
  • the tubing for the irrigation system,
  • a run-off plate that directs drip through water into...
  • the last acrylic box, which can be easily removed to be emptied,
  • all four plants.
Finally, rising above the base, there is a copper tube upon which the lights and light shade are mounted.

The plants reside on the top level, and below them are the water container and pump. Stored above the water in the cross between plants is the semi-waterproof electronics box that allows for easy portability and access to the arduinos and circuits controlling the sensor system. The tall copper pipe on which the lights rest and through which the light wires run, extends through the middle of the structure and above the plants.

Irrigation

The irrigation system is a series of pipes that run from the pump in the reservoir to each of the four plants. We use a small pump to pressurize the water, and from there it travels through a series of splitters up to the top of the structure. We also have analogue flow control valves and drip emitters that help us fine tune the flow rate to each plant.
