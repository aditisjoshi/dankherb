Mechanical System

Mechanical

Overview

The mechanical system for our autonomous garden consists mainly of the structure and the irrigation system. The structure contains the plants, houses electronics, and ensures that the LEDs and irrigation tubing/water reservoir are in the proper locations to perform their functions. The irrigation system delivers water to the plants, responding to sensor data about the soil’s condition.

We designed the project to be modular, easily configurable, and readily variable to allow for quick adaptation and universality of use. It is also intended to be long lasting (relative to most PoE projects), and we hope it’s life span extends into the coming months or years.

The mechanical system of Gardenai is designed to both support every other system and house all of it in a very space conscious and visually appealing form. Built mostly from laser cut MDF, it consists of a non-structural but aesthetically pleasing wrap around exterior, an interior framing, top and bottom, and three laser cut and cemented acrylic boxes. The MDF interior box serves to hold (1) all of our electronics high above the water in the first acrylic box, (2) fresh water ready to be pumped into the plants in box number two, (3) all of the tubing for the irrigation system, (4) a run-off plate that directs drip through water into (5) the last acrylic box, which can be easily removed to be emptied, and finally, (6) all four plants. Finally, rising above the base, there is a copper tube upon which the lights and light shade is mounted.

Structure

Made out of MDF, our structure is essentially a box with multiple compartments. On the top level the plants reside, and on the bottom is the water container and pump. Stored above the water in the cross between plants is the semi-waterproof electronics box that allows for easy portability and access to the arduinos and circuits controlling the sensor system. Additionally there is a tall copper pipe running through the middle of the structure and extending above the plants which enables the LEDs to be the proper distance away from the plants to avoid light burn.

Irrigation

The irrigation system is a series of pipes that run from the pump in the reservoir to each of the four plants. We use a small pump to pressurize the water, and from there it travels through a series of splitters up to the top of the structure. We also have analogue flow control valves and drip emitters that help us fine tune the flow rate to each plant.
